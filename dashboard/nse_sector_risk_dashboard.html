NSE Sector Rotation & Risk-Adjusted Return Dashboard

One year of daily price data across 30 NSE-listed stocks spanning 6 sectors, analyzed for return per unit of risk taken — not just which stocks went up the most.

Methodology note "Return per risk" is a simplified Sharpe-style ratio (total return ÷ annualized volatility, no risk-free rate subtracted) — a relative ranking tool for comparing stocks/sectors against each other in this dataset, not a formal investment metric. This is educational analysis, not investment advice.

Key findings

  • IT sector Every single IT stock in the basket (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCLTech, Tech Mahindra) posted a negative risk-adjusted return — the only sector where 100% of constituents underperformed.
  • Auto sector Eicher Motors (+35.5% return) and Bajaj Auto (+27.0%) drove Auto to the best sector-level risk-adjusted return, despite carrying the second-highest average volatility — high risk that actually paid off.
  • ITC (FMCG) Posted the single worst raw return (-30.7%) while carrying one of the lowest volatilities in the dataset (19.4%) — a poor outcome that can't be explained away as "high risk, high reward gone wrong."
  • Reliance India's largest company by market cap posted a negative return (-9.7%) over the period — a reminder that "blue chip" doesn't guarantee positive short-term performance.
